Panko Chicken Breast

Description

This is such a simple recipe but it's a big hit with my family. They ask me to make this often for them. We usually have it with mashed potatoes and a veggie for sides. It's also nice if you are on a budget since you don't need that many ingredients to make this.

I encourage you to experiment and explore different flavors with this recipe! It's really basic so it'd be really easy to customize if you want to add different seasonings or a sauce to go along with it.

If you end up liking it, I recommend getting one of those deep fryer appliances to save yourself some time. I don't really like messing around with hot oil, so the quicker, the better imo.

Ingredients

  • chicken breast ~ 5 pieces
  • all purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • panko breadcrumbs
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. optional: if thick pieces of chicken, I recommend to butterfly the chicken by just cutting it in half so that you can open it like a book.
  2. crack both eggs in a separate bowl and whisk the eggs until an even color and then set aside
  3. season the chicken with salt and pepper to your preference on both sides
  4. with a meat tenderizer, tenderize each side of the chicken. if you butterfly'd your piece, make sure you left it open so that the piece will be thinner
  5. cover chicken in a thin layer of flour
  6. dip chicken in egg mix until evenly coated
  7. cover chicken in panko breadcrumbs, make sure breadcrumbs are evenly spread with no gaps for better frying
  8. deep fry until panko is golden. keep in mind that larger pieces will take longer to cook than smaller pieces.

    oil temperature depends on your deep fryer. Mine allows for 3-4 minutes

    internal temperature of chicken should be 165 deg F or 73.9 deg C

  9. serve with your preferred sides. I also recommend this chicken with sriracha sauce for some spice!
